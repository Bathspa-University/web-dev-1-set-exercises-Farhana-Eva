Plot

Game of Thrones follows the noble families of Westeros as they vie for control of the Iron Throne and the Seven Kingdoms.Game of Thrones follows a large cast of characters and interwoven story arcs. It is primarily set on the fictional continent of Westeros, which is divided into the Seven Kingdoms and the lands in the far North beyond “the Wall,” an enormous wall of fortified ice. The Seven Kingdoms is a realm composed of seven once independent kingdoms that have been, to greater or lesser degrees, unified under a single monarch, who sits on the Iron Throne. The planet on which Westeros is found has seasons of variable lengths, with summers and winters often lasting for many years.The series focuses on several noble houses of Westeros and a complicated, multiparty war for the Iron Throne that features shifting conflicts, alliances, and betrayals. Key houses include the Starks, who rule in the North; the Lannisters, a wealthy family from the West who have married into the royal line; the Baratheons, who at the outset hold the Iron Throne; and the Targaryens, who once controlled the Iron Throne using dragons but who have been nearly wiped out by the time the story begins. This war develops after the death of King Robert Baratheon and the resulting feud between his friend and ally Lord Eddard Stark (Sean Bean) and Robert’s widow, Queen Cersei Lannister, and her children. The conflict draws in two of the Stark children, Robb and Sansa, while the younger children Arya and Bran find themselves on their own terrifying adventures. Jon Snow, raised as a bastard member of the Stark family, serves in the Night’s Watch, a military organization that defends the Seven Kingdoms from forces beyond the Wall. Cersei’s brothers Jaime and Tyrion (Peter Dinklage) also play key roles. Exiled on the continent of Essos, Daenerys Targaryen seeks to return to Westeros with dragons and an army in order to reclaim the Iron Throne for House Targaryen.

Overview

"Game of Thrones" is a fantasy drama television series created by David Benioff and D. B. Weiss. It is based on the "A Song of Ice and Fire" novels by George R. R. Martin.

The series is set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos and follows the power struggles among noble families as they vie for control of the Iron Throne and the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros.

For more information, visit the official website.

Characters

    Jon Snow: Bastard son of Eddard Stark, raised at Winterfell. Joins the Night's Watch and rises to prominence, eventually becoming a key player in the struggle against the White Walkers.

    Daenerys Targaryen: Exiled princess of the Targaryen dynasty, with a claim to the Iron Throne. Mother of dragons and breaker of chains, she seeks to reclaim her birthright and rule the Seven Kingdoms.

    Tyrion Lannister: The witty and intelligent youngest son of Tywin Lannister, often underestimated due to his stature. Known for his sharp tongue and strategic mind, he navigates the treacherous politics of King's Landing.

    Arya Stark: Spirited daughter of Eddard Stark, who trains as an assassin after witnessing her family's downfall. She embarks on a journey of revenge and self-discovery, becoming a skilled and deadly fighter.

    Sansa Stark: Eldest daughter of Eddard Stark, who endures immense suffering and manipulation but emerges as a resilient and shrewd player in the game of thrones.

    Cersei Lannister: Queen regent and later queen mother, known for her ruthless ambition and cunning schemes to maintain power for herself and her family.

    Jaime Lannister: Known as the Kingslayer for killing the Mad King Aerys Targaryen, he struggles with his identity and moral choices, evolving from a despised figure to a more complex character.

    Bran Stark: Crippled son of Eddard Stark who possesses mystical abilities, including the power of greensight. He becomes the Three-Eyed Raven, tasked with preserving the history of Westeros.

    Sandor "The Hound" Clegane: A fearsome warrior with a burned face, known for his brutal nature and conflicted morality. He forms an unlikely bond with Arya Stark during their travels

    Petyr "Littlefinger" Baelish: Master manipulator and schemer, who rises from humble beginnings to become a powerful player in the politics of Westeros. He orchestrates chaos to further his own ambitions.
